USA - EB5 investment visa

overview of EB5 investment visa program

The EB-5, is the fifth employment-based visa intended to encourage the flow of US capital Economy and to promote employment for US Citizens. To accomplish the above goals the foreign investors may obtain immigration benefits to make an investment. This programme mandates a minimum capital that foreign investors must contribute as well as a mandates of 10 full-time jobs must be created on account of each investment. In addition to the return the investors can achieve , foreign investors and their qualifying family members are offered the prospect, but the lawful permanent residence in the United States is not guaranteed.

there are three basic requiments for an EB-5 visa

  • .The Investor must establish a business or invest in an existing business that was already created or restructured since November 19, 1990.
  • The Investor must have invested USD $1,000,000 (only USD $500,000 while investing in a USCIS designated regional centre) in business.
  • .Its mandate that the business must create full-time employment for at least 10 US workers.

what are some of the benifits of the EB-5 visa program?

  • Qualifying Investors and their spouse and minor children (under age 21) will be granted US Lawful Permanent Residency (GREEN CARD)
  • Investor and family may live/work anywhere in the United States. Minor Children may obtain employment, subject to age, state and governmental laws and restrictions.
  • Educational benefits include admission to Universities at US residents costs.
  • The EB-5 program does not require immigrant investors to manage their investment on a day to day basis.
  • .After completing 5 years, the investor and their family may obtain US citizenship, subject to meeting all immigration requirements, as per US Law.

how long must i remain in the united states each year?

You are supposed to remain in the US for more than 180 days (6 months) each year. Other wise the permanent residence status will be deemed to have abandoned .

what is the difference between "conditional" and "unconditional" green cards?

Under the regulations, an investor who has been approved for the EB-5 immigrant visa receives a “conditional” green card, which will be reissued after two years, subject to removal of conditions. Otherwise, both the cards offer the same rights and privileges.
